BENZENE

Benzene is a crucial chemical building block and it is a component of many chemical compounds. Also, it has several commercial uses and demand. Producing of Styrene monomer is the largest use of benzene, followed by cumene/phenol, cyclohexane and nitrobenzene. These products are used to manufacture a wide range of plastics, fibers, resin and films. Benzene is also used as a major part of gasoline, and it is an excellent solvent for waxes, resins, gums, rubber and various other organic materials. S-Chem is one of the largest producers of Benzene in the middle east.

Aromax® Technology

S-Chem utilize Chevron Phillips's Aromax® Technology which the premier on-purpose benzene production technology. This technology is providing a superior ability to convert light hydrocarbons to aromatics. The Aromax® process is basically convert light paraffins and naphthene to hydrogen and aromatic products using conventional fixed-bed reforming equipment. The following are facts about the Aromax® Process:
  • First reforming process based on a zeolitic catalyst.
  • Best suited for converting C6-C8 hydrocarbons.
  • Exceptional selectivity for converting C6 and C7 paraffins and naphthenes to benzene, toluene and hydrogen.
  • Toluene extraction may or may not be required.
  • Proprietary catalyst technology.

Advantages of Aromax® Technology

  • High yield of benzene, toluene, and hydrogen from C6/C7 naphtha.
  • Enables the use of lower-priced paraffinic naphtha.
  • Sulfur and carburization control.
  • More than 16 years of commercial experience.
  • Proprietary sulfur and carburization control technology.

CYCLOHEXANE

Cyclohexane is an alicyclic hydrocarbon comprising a ring of six carbon atoms; the cyclic form of hexane, used as a precursor to manufacture nylon 6 and 6,6. It also has a role as a non-polar solvent, industrial catalyst carrier and process diluent. Cyclohexane is considered as cycloalkane, colorless and a volatile organic compound.
